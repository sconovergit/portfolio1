Alex Cooper Photography

Amateur photography website meant to showcase the photographers portfolio as well as allowing him to get more clients and give them a central location for all of their photography needs.

Development | Research | Design

Problem

Alex had no way of getting new clients or a way to manage current clients.

Amateur photographer did not have a website and made a lot more work for himself by doing every step of the process. He needed a way to automate some of his services like giving the client the finished product and offering them a way to have prints and the digital copies.

Solution

A simple website for his potential and current clients to see his work, book him, and purchase their pictures.

A website that is simple for himself and his clients to use while being capable of booking new clients and being able to interact with them.

User Surveys

Get to know our users.

I used these surveys to see exactly what the user wanted out of a photography website. Through this survey I learned that most users want to know an estimate of a price before they even take the time to contact the photographer. I also learned the amount of photos most people wanted as well as what types of photos they wanted.

Competitive Analysis

What's everyone else doing?

The cloud storage market does not have a lot of competitors and seems ready for an application like WOLF. Google Drive, Pinterest, and Instagram were the 3 biggest platforms with similarities to WOLF. I learned that I was going to have to incorporate every single feature from these companies into WOLF if it was going to be a success.

Peter Mckinnon

Peter Mckinnon is a very popular youtuber/photographer/videographer. He has dedicated his career to his art and although he no longer does private photos for people, he is still an aspirational competitor. His website is user friendly but does not have much on it. It seems to be mostly used for a way to contact him or buy merchandise.

Angela Clifton

Tampa photographer that has been doing photography for a long time and makes her living off of private shoots from clients. The website has a lot of her pictures as well as testimonials. She only does photography in Tampa so this limits her market.

User Flows

Which path to take?

I used User Flows to make sure I was keeping the site simplistic but elegant. I wanted the least amount of clicks possible to get to where the user wanted to go.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

From Sketching to XD.

I drew low fidelity wireframes for desktop and mobile to visualize how I wanted Alex's site to be organized. I then used these sketches to create digital wireframes to get a more exact feel of the proportions and to start setting up a visual hierarchy.

Usability Testing

What needs to change?

I tested 3 users about Alex's photography site so far. The first question was having them navigate to the contact page. All 3 did it with ease since it's on the side nav. The next question was having them to preview some of Alex's work. After clicking on portfolio they figured out that it was broken up into different categories. The final task was to contact Alex. Since this too was on the navigation they did not have a difficult time with it at all.

Development

Coding With a Grid.

After finishing all of my testing, I had to move the design into the development process. I had never used a grid layout before and wanted to push myself and learn something new so I decided to make all of the photos on the site using grid.

What I've Learned

Never Stop Learning.

This was a fairly simple project and I wanted to push myself to learn as much as possible while designing this project. I forced myself to learn about developing with a grid and learned a lot about using pictures on websites and how to reduce their overal impact on load times.
