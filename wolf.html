WOLF

Cloud storage application that combines content creation, storage management, and communication through a custom messenger.

Design | Research | Branding

Problem

Using multiple applications slows workflow and lowers efficiency.

An indecisive client wanted to enter the cloud storage market and create an application that had multiple uses to make a teams workflow easier while also having a cloud space to store assets.

Solution

A multifaceted application capable of creating and sharing content, managing storage, and communicating through messenger.

A design that incorporates an open layout that keeps everything the user needs within a single click, allowing them to manipulate their assets as needed and reach out to fellow colleagues quickly.

User Surveys

Get to know our users

I used surveys to limit the scope of what users wanted. This step showed me that uploading, creating, sharing, storing content, and collaboration were the most important features for users. It also showed me that most users were going to be very familiar with computers and how applications work.

Competitive Analysis

What's everyone else doing?

The cloud storage market does not have a lot of competitors and seems ready for an application like WOLF. Google Drive, Pinterest, and Instagram were the 3 biggest platforms with similarities to WOLF. I learned that I was going to have to incorporate every single feature from these companies into WOLF if it was going to be a success.

Google Drive

Strong, prominent company with lots of features and ways to create content. Very organized interface and easy to use. Capable of interconnectivity between apps and their only competition has only recently entered the market.

Instagram

Free to use, extremely popular social media app. Minimal options for content creation but very easy to connect with people and make connections. Organization is hard to use and there are lots of competitors already in the market.

Pinterest

Unique app that is very simple to use and organize. Few features and becomes repetitive quickly with a limited market. Few competitors but current competitors have very similar features.

User Personas

Users First

I collected a lot of research on the potential user as well as my competitors. I combined all of this into User Personas to help refine my requirements as well as to help me focus on designing to solve these users problems. Having this template of users that I wanted to target helped me design with these users in mind.

Alex Cooper

Engineer

Age: 31 | Location: Tampa | Gender: Male

Motivation

Alex currently uses Pinterest and Instagram for ideas for DIY projects for their house and loves to share them with his partner. He uses Google Drive a lot for work but has to use multiple apps to create the content that he wants in his buckets. Alex could benefit from our app by combining the sharing and sorting capabilities of all 3 apps as well as being able to create content, edit, save, and collaborate with his team.

Pain Points

His work flow is much slower when having to use multiple applications.

Zach Davis

Residence Director

Age: 34 | Location: Napa | Gender: Male

Motivation

Zach currently only uses Pinterest and Instagram to blow off steam. Google Drive is something he does not use, and instead uses Excel spreadsheets and Word documents. If anyone on his team wants to add or edit any sort of document they have to go through Zach. Zach could benefit from WOLF by allowing the team to edit documents and free up time for him.

Pain Points

He must currently edit documents all by himself and has no capability to allow the team to create documents.

Marc Trujillo

Student

Age: 19 | Location: Tampa | Gender: Male

Motivation

Marc only uses social media and apps like Pinterest if he just so happens to have a few spare minutes in his day. He currently does not use Google Drive since he does not have a lot of time to learn anything other than his nursing studies. Marc could benefit from WOLF because it can help him create and store all of his files in one location and not be at risk of losing them.

Pain Points

Currently stores files on his local computer, running the risk of losing them.

Which path to take?

Low Fidelity Wireframes

From Sketching to InVision

I drew up low fidelity wireframes to begin visualizing the WOLF concept. This helped me figure out what I wanted to include in WOLF and the order in which I wanted to lay out the dashboard and homepage. Using these wireframes I learned exactly where I wanted buttons and what to emphasize.

Usability Testing

What needs to change?

I tested 3 users and asked them some questions about WOLF so far. In this first round I asked the participants about the homepage, to sign up for an account, add a piece of content, and to organize a piece of content. There were some sizing issues and a few incorrect paths. Some features that I found worked very well were the menu for adding content being easy to find, the ease of saving content, and the obvious call to action buttons.

Branding

Simple, Professional, Reliable, Teamwork

These words are the branding focus. From the logo to the colors and fonts, I made sure everything encompassed these words. WOLF has a professional feel and the logo has the feeling of confidence and simplicity and portrays the pack mentality of a wolf.

Design Iterations

If at first you don't succeed, try, try again

There is always something new to learn. Getting from sketches to this part was no easy task, but I was finally ready to try testing on users again. Visualizing the journey helped me to better understand what needed to change.

Prototyping and Testing

Now what?

I performed another usability test for the same 3 tasks. After this round I needed to tone down some colors on the homepage and add some visual hierarchy. From here I performed a preference test of a few different graphics for the homepage of WOLF.

What I've Learned

A project is never really over

I have learned a lot throughout this project. I have learned to test my designs multiple times with users and the power of simplicity and white space. I also learned that there is always something to learn and a new way to grow.
